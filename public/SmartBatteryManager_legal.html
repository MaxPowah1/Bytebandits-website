Privacy Policy

Last Updated: November 2025

1. Introduction

Smart Battery Manager ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Data Collected by the App

Our app collects the following information exclusively on your device:

Battery Metrics:

  • Battery level (percentage)
  • Battery temperature
  • Voltage readings
  • Current flow (charge/discharge)
  • Charging status and source (AC, USB, Wireless)
  • Battery health indicators
  • Battery technology information

Device State:

  • Screen on/off status
  • Power save mode status
  • Thermal status
  • Device idle mode

App Usage Statistics (Optional):

  • App usage data (only if you grant "Usage Access" permission)
  • Used to identify which apps consume the most battery

2.2 Data Storage

All data is stored locally on your device only. We do not:

  • Transmit any data to external servers
  • Share data with third parties
  • Use analytics or tracking services
  • Collect personal identification information

3. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA), we process your data based on:

  • Legitimate Interest: Battery monitoring is essential for the app's core functionality
  • Consent: App usage statistics are only collected with your explicit permission

4. How We Use Your Information

The collected data is used solely for:

  • Displaying battery status and health information
  • Providing insights about battery usage patterns
  • Calculating battery cycles and wear estimates
  • Showing charging efficiency and patterns
  • Identifying power-consuming apps (if usage access is granted)

5. Data Retention

Data is retained on your device as long as:

  • The app is installed on your device
  • You have not cleared the app's data through device settings

You can delete all collected data at any time by:

  • Clearing the app's data in your device settings
  • Uninstalling the app

If you are located in the EEA, you have the following rights:

  • Right to Access: Request information about the data we have collected
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate data

  • Right to Restrict Processing: Limit how we process your data

  • Opt-out of the sale of personal information (we do not sell data)

8. Children's Privacy

9. Third-Party Services
